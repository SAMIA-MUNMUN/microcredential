Ka'bah

Ka'bah is one of the main places to visit in Saudi Arabia, which is located in the city of Makkah. Makkah, in a desert valley in western Saudi Arabia, is Islam’s holiest city, as it’s the birthplace of the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) and the faith itself. Millions of muslims arrive there for the annual Hajj (pilgrimage) and also for Umrah. Dating from the 7th century, the central Masjid al-Haram (Sacred Mosque) surrounds the Kaaba, The Great Mosque of Makkah, known as the Masjid al-Haram, is home to the Ka'bah, believed by Muslims to have been built by Abraham and Ishmael. It is one of Islam's holiest sites and the direction of prayer for all Muslims (qibla).

Masjid An Nawawi

Masjid An Nawawi is the second largest mosque and second holiest site in Islam. It is located in the heart of Medina,Saudi Arabia. Every minaret, pulpit, door, wall, and window inside Masjid Nabawi has a story behind it. A tale that tells you about the roles and sacrifices of the companions of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) in the establishment of the Islamic community. if you are planning to go to Madinah, make sure to visit Riyadhul Jannah, The Praying Spot of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), Rawda and all the important places inside Masjid Nabawi.

King Fahd's Fountain

The fountain was donated to the city of Jeddah by King Fahd, hence its name. It was constructed between 1980 and 1983 and was launched in 1985.Located on the west coast of Saudi Arabia, the fountain jets water to a maximum height of 260 metres (853 ft). One can make a night-time trip to enjoy the illuminated spectacle of the world's tallest fountain.
